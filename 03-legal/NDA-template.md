## 1. Purpose and Asymmetric Protection

The Parties wish to explore and engage in a website design and development project ("Project"). In connection with the Project, each Party may disclose confidential information to the other.

This is a **mutual** non-disclosure agreement: either Party may disclose information. It is **not** a mutual agreement of equal burden. The Company is the owner of the Project, its systems, data, and business information. **The Vendor bears the primary and stricter confidentiality obligations** with respect to Company Confidential Information. The Company receives broader rights to retain and use Vendor submissions for procurement, evaluation, and business records, as set out in this Agreement.

---

## 2. Confidential Information

"Confidential Information" means any non-public information disclosed by one Party (the "Disclosing Party") to the other Party (the "Receiving Party"), whether oral, written, or digital, including but not limited to:

- Business strategies, plans, and financial data
- Website architecture, source code, and technical specifications
- Customer data, booking data, and CRM records
- API credentials, access tokens, and system credentials
- Design assets, brand materials, and content
- Proposals, pricing, staffing plans, methodologies, and bid materials
- Any information marked "Confidential" or that a reasonable person would understand to be confidential given the context

**Company Confidential Information** includes all WindhorseTour materials, systems, data, credentials, designs, code, customer information, and any other non-public information the Company discloses or makes accessible in connection with the Project.

**Vendor Confidential Information** includes non-public proposal content, commercial terms, team information, technical approaches, and other non-public information the Vendor shares in connection with the Project. Vendor Confidential Information does **not** include information the Company is entitled to obtain, retain, and analyze under **Section 5**.

---

## 3. Obligations

### 3.1 Vendor obligations (Company Confidential Information)

With respect to Company Confidential Information, the Vendor agrees to:

1. Keep all Company Confidential Information strictly confidential
2. Use Company Confidential Information **solely** for evaluating, negotiating, or performing work on the Project — and for no other client, project, product, or purpose
3. Not disclose Company Confidential Information to any third party without the Company's **prior written consent**
4. Not copy, download, export, screenshot, record, or store Company Confidential Information except as strictly necessary for the Project and only on systems approved for that purpose
5. Limit access to Company Confidential Information to employees or subcontractors who have a need to know, who are disclosed to the Company if requested, and who are bound by obligations at least as protective as this Agreement
6. Not use Company Confidential Information in portfolios, case studies, marketing, sales materials, conferences, or public demonstrations without the Company's **prior written approval**
7. Promptly notify the Company of any unauthorized disclosure, access, loss, or suspected breach
8. Cooperate with the Company's instructions regarding access controls, credential handling, and secure deletion

### 3.2 Company obligations (Vendor Confidential Information)

With respect to Vendor Confidential Information, the Company agrees to:

1. Keep identifiable Vendor Confidential Information confidential, except as permitted under **Section 5**
2. Use Vendor Confidential Information for procurement, evaluation, negotiation, contract administration, audit, dispute resolution, and related business purposes for the Project
3. Not disclose identifiable Vendor Confidential Information to competing bidders, except as required by law or with the Vendor's prior written consent
4. Limit internal access to employees and advisors who have a need to know

The Company's obligations under this Section 3.2 are subject to the Company's rights under **Section 5** and do not restrict the Company's ability to obtain, retain, archive, or analyze Vendor submissions.

---

## 4. Exclusions

This Agreement does not apply to information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate:

- Is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the Receiving Party
- Was already known to the Receiving Party prior to disclosure, as evidenced by written records created before receipt
- Is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to Confidential Information
- Is required to be disclosed by law or court order, provided the Receiving Party gives the Disclosing Party prompt prior written notice and cooperates with any protective measures

The Vendor may not rely on an exclusion for Company Confidential Information unless it provides satisfactory written evidence to the Company.

---

## 5. Company Archival and Analysis Rights

Notwithstanding any other provision of this Agreement, the Vendor acknowledges that WindhorseTour conducts formal procurement for the Project and must retain and analyze vendor submissions for business, legal, and compliance purposes.

The Company shall have the right to:

1. **Obtain and retain** Vendor Confidential Information disclosed in connection with the Project, including proposals, pricing, technical approaches, staffing information, correspondence, and related materials
2. **Archive** such information in WindhorseTour's records, whether or not the Vendor is selected for the Project
3. **Analyze** such information internally for bid evaluation, comparison of proposals, selection decisions, contract negotiation, audit trails, dispute resolution, and compliance with WindhorseTour's procurement process

The Company shall continue to protect identifiable Vendor Confidential Information in accordance with **Section 3.2**. The Company may use aggregated or anonymized information derived from Vendor submissions for internal reporting and process improvement.

This Section 5 does not grant the Company any license to use Vendor intellectual property, software, or proprietary tools except as strictly necessary for archival, analysis, and evaluation under this Agreement.

---

## 6. Vendor Retention Disclosure (Required at Signing)

The Vendor shall **not** retain any Company Confidential Information after the Project ends, discussions terminate, or the Company requests return or destruction, **unless** the Vendor has disclosed that retention in writing **before or at the time of signing** this Agreement.

At signing, the Vendor must complete **Appendix A — Vendor Retention Disclosure** below. That appendix must state, for each category of Company Confidential Information the Vendor may retain:

1. **What** may be retained (specific categories only — not "all materials")
2. **Why** retention is necessary (specific legal, regulatory, or operational reason)
3. **Where** it will be stored
4. **Who** will have access
5. **For how long** it will be retained
6. **How** it will be protected and eventually destroyed

If Appendix A is blank, marked "None," or left incomplete, the Vendor has **no right to retain** Company Confidential Information for any reason other than a **compulsory legal hold** that the Vendor notifies the Company of in writing within **five (5) business days**, identifying the applicable law, authority, and scope.

The Company is not required to approve Appendix A. Signing this Agreement does not constitute Company consent to any retention not expressly required by applicable law.

---

## 7. Return or Destruction of Information

### 7.1 Vendor return/destruction of Company Confidential Information

Upon written request by the Company, or upon termination of discussions or Project work, the Vendor shall **within ten (10) business days**:

1. Return or permanently destroy **all** Company Confidential Information and copies, including files, exports, backups, notes, screenshots, local repositories, credentials, and materials held by subcontractors
2. Delete Company Confidential Information from all devices, cloud services, email, chat, ticket systems, and AI tools used in connection with the Project
3. Revoke and cease use of any credentials, tokens, or access granted by the Company
4. Certify in writing, signed by an authorized officer, that the Vendor has complied with this Section 7.1

The Vendor may retain Company Confidential Information **only** to the extent expressly listed in a completed Appendix A, or under a compulsory legal hold disclosed under **Section 6**. Automatic backup, disaster recovery, convenience copies, model-training caches, and "internal compliance" archives are **not** permitted retention grounds unless expressly disclosed in Appendix A.

### 7.2 Company retention of Vendor Confidential Information

The Company is **not** required to return or destroy Vendor Confidential Information submitted in connection with the Project. The Company may retain and use such information as permitted under **Section 5**.

### 7.3 Survival

The Vendor's obligations under Sections 3.1, 6, and 7.1 survive termination of this Agreement.

---

## 8. Intellectual Property and No License

Nothing in this Agreement grants the Vendor any license or rights in Company Confidential Information, WindhorseTour intellectual property, trademarks, designs, code, data, or systems, except the limited right to use Company Confidential Information strictly as necessary to evaluate, negotiate, or perform the Project.

All work product created by the Vendor for the Project is and will remain the exclusive property of the Company unless otherwise agreed in a separate written agreement signed by the Company. The Vendor shall not assert ownership, reuse, or commercial exploitation rights over Company materials or Project work product.

Pre-existing intellectual property of each Party remains the property of that Party.

---

## 9. Term

This Agreement is effective as of the Effective Date and continues for **two (2) years**, unless terminated earlier by either Party upon thirty (30) days written notice.

Confidentiality obligations survive termination for an additional **five (5) years** with respect to Company Confidential Information, and **three (3) years** with respect to Vendor Confidential Information, except that the Company's rights under **Section 5** may continue for as long as WindhorseTour reasonably requires for legal, compliance, and business-record purposes.

---

## 10. Remedies

The Vendor acknowledges that breach of this Agreement, especially unauthorized retention, disclosure, or use of Company Confidential Information, may cause irreparable harm to the Company for which monetary damages would be inadequate.

The Company shall be entitled to seek injunctive or other equitable relief without the requirement to post a bond, in addition to all other remedies available at law or in equity. The Vendor shall indemnify the Company for direct damages, reasonable legal fees, and remediation costs arising from the Vendor's breach of Sections 3.1, 6, 7.1, or 8.

---
