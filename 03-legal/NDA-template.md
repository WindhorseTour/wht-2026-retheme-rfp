## 1. Purpose

The Parties wish to explore and engage in a website design and development project ("Project"). In connection with the Project, each Party may disclose confidential information to the other. This Agreement governs the protection of that information and the limited rights each Party has to use it.

---

## 2. Confidential Information

"Confidential Information" means any non-public information disclosed by one Party (the "Disclosing Party") to the other Party (the "Receiving Party"), whether oral, written, or digital, including but not limited to:

- Business strategies, plans, and financial data
- Website architecture, source code, and technical specifications
- Customer data, booking data, and CRM records
- API credentials, access tokens, and system credentials
- Design assets, brand materials, and content
- Proposals, pricing, staffing plans, methodologies, and bid materials
- Any information marked "Confidential" or that a reasonable person would understand to be confidential given the context

Confidential Information disclosed by the Company includes WindhorseTour materials, systems, and data. Confidential Information disclosed by the Vendor includes proposal content, commercial terms, team information, technical approaches, and any other non-public information the Vendor shares in connection with the Project.

---

## 3. Obligations

Each Receiving Party agrees to:

1. Keep the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information strictly confidential
2. Use Confidential Information solely for the purpose of evaluating, negotiating, or performing work on the Project
3. Not disclose Confidential Information to any third party without prior written consent from the Disclosing Party, except as permitted in Section 6
4. Limit access to Confidential Information to employees or subcontractors who have a need to know and are bound by obligations at least as protective as this Agreement
5. Promptly notify the Disclosing Party of any unauthorized disclosure or suspected breach

---

## 4. Exclusions

This Agreement does not apply to information that:

- Is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the Receiving Party
- Was already known to the Receiving Party prior to disclosure, as evidenced by written records
- Is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of Confidential Information
- Is required to be disclosed by law or court order, provided the Receiving Party gives the Disclosing Party prompt prior written notice and cooperates with any protective measures

---

## 5. Company Archival and Analysis Rights

Notwithstanding any other provision of this Agreement, the Vendor acknowledges that WindhorseTour conducts formal procurement for the Project and must retain and analyze vendor submissions for business, legal, and compliance purposes.

The Company shall have the right to:

1. **Obtain and retain** Confidential Information disclosed by the Vendor in connection with the Project, including proposals, pricing, technical approaches, staffing information, correspondence, and related materials
2. **Archive** such information in WindhorseTour's records, whether or not the Vendor is selected for the Project
3. **Analyze** such information internally for bid evaluation, comparison of proposals, selection decisions, contract negotiation, audit trails, dispute resolution, and compliance with WindhorseTour's procurement process

The Company shall continue to protect Vendor Confidential Information retained under this Section in accordance with this Agreement. The Company shall not disclose identifiable Vendor Confidential Information to competing bidders, except as required by law or with the Vendor's prior written consent. The Company may use aggregated or anonymized information derived from Vendor submissions for internal reporting and process improvement.

This Section 5 does not grant the Company any license to use Vendor intellectual property, software, or proprietary tools except as strictly necessary for archival, analysis, and evaluation under this Agreement.

---

## 6. Return or Destruction of Information

Upon written request by a Disclosing Party, or upon termination of discussions relating to the Project, each Receiving Party shall promptly return or destroy the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information and any copies thereof, and certify in writing that it has done so, **except**:

- The Company may retain copies of Vendor Confidential Information as permitted under **Section 5**
- Either Party may retain copies to the extent required by law, regulation, or bona fide internal compliance or backup policies, provided such retained copies remain subject to this Agreement

---

## 7. Intellectual Property

Nothing in this Agreement grants either Party any license or rights in the other Party's Confidential Information beyond what is strictly necessary to evaluate, negotiate, or perform the Project. All work product created by the Vendor for the Project remains the property of the Company unless otherwise agreed in a separate written agreement. Pre-existing intellectual property of each Party remains the property of that Party.

---

## 8. Term

This Agreement is effective as of the Effective Date and continues for **two (2) years**, unless terminated earlier by either Party upon thirty (30) days written notice. Confidentiality obligations survive termination for an additional **three (3) years**, except that the Company's rights under **Section 5** to retain and analyze Vendor Confidential Information for archival and procurement records may continue for as long as WindhorseTour reasonably requires for legal, compliance, and business-record purposes.

---

## 9. Remedies

Each Party acknowledges that breach of this Agreement may cause irreparable harm for which monetary damages would be inadequate. The non-breaching Party shall be entitled to seek injunctive or other equitable relief without the requirement to post a bond, in addition to all other remedies available at law.

---

## 10. Governing Law

This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the People's Republic of China. Any disputes shall be resolved in the courts of Chengdu, Sichuan, China.

---

## 11. Entire Agreement

This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties with respect to its subject matter and supersedes all prior discussions and agreements. It may be amended only in writing signed by both Parties.
