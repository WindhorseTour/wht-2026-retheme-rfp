In-Page CTA Blocks — Inline Components

Reusable blocks embedded inside planning articles and destination guides. Never a popup — they live in the page. Title, body text, and button copy are swappable per context. Each button links to either Form 1 (Contact popup) or Form 2 (Plan My Trip page).

Inline / reusable
⚡

Placement: Inside article content at natural decision points — after a cost section, after a permit explanation, at the end of an itinerary guide. Button A opens the Contact Us popup (Form 1). Button B navigates to Plan My Trip page (Form 2).

A — Mid-article callout · left orange border · button → opens Contact Us popup (Form 1)

The Tibet Travel Permit (TTP) is the main permit required for all foreign travelers to Tibet. It's issued by the Tibet Tourism Bureau and must be arranged through a registered Tibet travel agency — individual travelers cannot apply directly.

Need help with permits?

Our team in Lhasa handles all Tibet permits for you. Tell us your travel dates and we'll start the process — permits typically take 2–3 weeks.

In addition to the TTP, travelers visiting certain areas such as Everest Base Camp or Mount Kailash will need additional area permits, which your agency will arrange at the same time.

B — Guide hub page banner · full-width · always present on Tibet / China guide hub pages · button → Plan My Trip page (Form 2)
