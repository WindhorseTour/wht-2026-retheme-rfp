Itinerary: 5 Days Lhasa and Yamdrok Lake Tour

Arrival in Lhasa

Tibet plateau · arrival

Your Tibetan guide will greet you at Lhasa Gonggar Airport or the Lhasa Railway Station upon arrival and transfer you to your hotel. The drive from the airport takes 1–1.5 hours; from the train station around 20–30 minutes.

After checking in, the rest of the day is free to rest and acclimatize to Lhasa's altitude (3,650 m / 12,000 ft).

Drepung & Sera Monasteries

Sera Monastery · monk debate

In the morning, visit Drepung Monastery, once the world's largest monastery, home to more than 10,000 monks at its peak.

In the afternoon, explore Sera Monastery, famous for its daily monk debates (3–5pm, except Sundays).

In the evening, enjoy a welcome dinner with your group.

Lhasa city highlights

Potala Palace · Lhasa

Start with Potala Palace, Tibet's most iconic landmark and former winter residence of the Dalai Lamas.

Continue to Jokhang Temple, Tibet's most sacred temple and spiritual heart of Lhasa.

Walk Barkhor Street, the ancient kora path where pilgrims spin prayer wheels and perform prostrations.

End the day at a traditional Tibetan sweet tea house, sipping butter tea among locals.

Yamdrok Lake day trip

Yamdrok Lake · LuRila viewpoint

Drive to Yamdrok Lake (Yamdrok Tso), one of Tibet's three sacred lakes, with turquoise waters ringed by snow-capped peaks and grazing yaks.

Stop at Lurila viewpoint (4,700 m) for panoramic views of the entire lake and Noijin Kangsang Peak (7,191 m).

Visit Rituo Monastery on the lakeshore peninsula, and share a simple lakeside lunch with a local Tibetan family.

Return to Lhasa via Gampala Pass (4,900 m) with one final sweeping view of Yamdrok.

Departure

Your guide and driver will transfer you to Lhasa Gonggar Airport or the Train Station. Free airport shuttle at 6:30am, 8:00am, and 12:00pm. Train station drop-off complimentary at any time.
