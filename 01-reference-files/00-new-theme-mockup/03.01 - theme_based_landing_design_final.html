Tibet festival tour FAQs

What travelers ask most before booking a festival trip.

March to October covers most major celebrations. Spring brings the Nyingchi Peach Blossom Festival; summer brings Saga Dawa and the horse-racing festivals; August is the grand Shoton Festival in Lhasa. Winter offers Losar (Tibetan New Year) and the Butter Lamp Festival with far fewer tourists.
Book at least 2–3 months ahead for major festivals like Shoton, Saga Dawa and the horse races, and 3–4 months for remote Kham and Amdo festivals such as Monlam and Yushu, where accommodation is limited. We handle all permits and logistics.
Foreign travelers cannot travel independently in Tibet. You must join an organized tour with a licensed agency, travel with a registered Tibetan guide, and hold a Tibet Travel Permit — all of which we arrange for every festival tour.
Saga Dawa marks Buddha's birth, enlightenment and passing, usually in May or June. At Mount Kailash thousands of pilgrims walk the sacred 52km kora — the most auspicious time to make the circuit, especially in the 2026 Year of the Horse.
Not directly, but long hours outdoors and crowds can cause overexertion. Our itineraries build in gradual acclimatization, and vehicles and most hotels carry oxygen. Rest, hydrate, and avoid alcohol in your first 48 hours.
Yes — Shoton, Saga Dawa and Nagchu fall in peak season (May–October), so tours, hotels and train tickets cost more and key sites get busy. Booking 2–3 months ahead secures good guides and accommodation.
