Can you travel to Tibet?

Yes — Tibet is open to international tourists in 2026 and tours are operating normally. However, independent travel is not permitted. All foreign visitors must book a pre-arranged tour with a licensed local agency, which provides a licensed guide, private vehicle, and all required permits.

This applies to every destination in the Tibet Autonomous Region (TAR), including Lhasa, Everest Base Camp, and Mount Kailash. The requirement has been in place for decades and is unlikely to change.

Permits required

Tibet requires several permits, all of which are arranged by your licensed travel agency. You cannot apply for them independently.

Tibet Travel Permit (TTP)

The main permit, issued by the Tibet Tourism Bureau. Required for all foreign visitors to the TAR. Processing takes approximately 2–3 weeks and must be arranged before you arrive in China.

Additional permits

Depending on your destinations, you may also need:

  • Alien Travel Permit (ATP) — for areas outside Lhasa city
  • Military Permit — for border areas including Everest Base Camp
  • Frontier Pass — for areas near Nepal and Bhutan borders

Best time to visit Tibet

The best time to visit Tibet is from April to October. The most popular months are May, June, September, and October — mild temperatures, clear skies, and manageable crowds.

  • April – June: warming up, clear views, spring festivals. Good for EBC.
  • July – August: peak season, warmer but more rain. Some roads may be affected.
  • September – October: best overall weather, clear skies, autumn colours.
  • November – March: cold but possible. 30–50% cheaper. Kailash and EBC have limited access.
Our recommendation

Late September is our favourite time for most first-time visitors. Crowds drop sharply after China's Golden Week, skies are exceptionally clear, and temperatures are comfortable at altitude. If you're planning EBC, aim for late April to mid-May — the mountain is clear before the monsoon clouds arrive.

How to get to Tibet

There are three main ways to reach Tibet from mainland China:

By flight

Direct flights to Lhasa Gonggar Airport from Beijing, Chengdu, Shanghai, Xining, and other major cities. Flight time from Chengdu is around 2.5 hours. This is the most common option.

By train

The Qinghai–Tibet Railway is one of the world's most scenic rail journeys. The Lhasa Express departs from Beijing, Chengdu, Shanghai, Guangzhou, and Xining. The journey from Chengdu takes approximately 36 hours. Oxygen is supplied onboard to help with altitude.

Overland from Nepal

Entry via the Gyirong border crossing from Kathmandu is possible with the appropriate permits. This route is popular for travelers doing a Tibet–Nepal combination.

Local tip

We recommend flying into Lhasa and taking the train back — you get the dramatic arrival by air and the scenic Qinghai–Tibet Railway on the return. Book soft sleeper class from Xining for the best experience. Our team arranges train tickets as part of your tour package.

How many days do you need?

Most travelers spend 8 to 14 days in Tibet, depending on their destinations and acclimatization needs.

  • 4–5 days: Classic Lhasa tour only
  • 8 days: Lhasa + Everest Base Camp (most popular)
  • 7 days: Lhasa to Kathmandu overland via Friendship Highway
  • 15 days: Mount Kailash circuit

Always allow at least 2 full days in Lhasa at the start of your trip for acclimatization before travelling to higher altitudes.

Group tour vs private tour

Both are legitimate options. The right choice depends on your budget, travel style, and group size.

Group tours are more affordable and great for solo travelers or couples. Groups are capped at 12 people. Departures are fixed, so you must match your dates to available slots.

Private tours offer full flexibility — your own dates, pace, and itinerary. For groups of 4 or more, private tour costs are comparable to group pricing. Recommended for families, special interests, and off-the-beaten-track routes.

Our honest view

Altitude & acclimatization

Altitude sickness is a real consideration for Tibet travel. Lhasa sits at 3,650m — higher than any point in the Alps. EBC is at 5,200m.

Key tips:

  • Spend at least 2 full days in Lhasa before travelling to higher altitudes
  • Stay hydrated and avoid alcohol for the first 48 hours
  • Walk slowly and rest frequently on your first day
  • Consult your doctor about Diamox (acetazolamide) before departure
  • Tell your guide immediately if you experience persistent headache, vomiting, or difficulty breathing
