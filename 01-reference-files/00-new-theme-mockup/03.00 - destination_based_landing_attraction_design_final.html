Lhasa to Everest Base Camp: private & group tours

WindhorseTour has run Tibet Everest Base Camp tours since 2007. Every trip includes an English-speaking local Tibetan guide, private vehicle and all required permits, with both private and small-group departures year-round. Tours run 7 to 15 days, starting in Lhasa before heading west along the Friendship Highway to the north face of Everest at 5,200m. The clearest views come from April to mid-June and September to November.

  • Lhasa — Potala Palace, Jokhang and the great monasteries
  • Yamdrok & Gyantse — sacred turquoise lake and the Kumbum stupa
  • Shigatse — Tashilhunpo, the last town before Everest
  • Everest Base Camp — the north face at 5,200m, reachable by road
  • Overland to Nepal — continue the Friendship Highway to Kathmandu

Tibet Everest Base Camp tour FAQs

What travelers ask most before booking an EBC trip.

April to mid-June and September to November offer the most stable weather and the clearest views of the north face. These are the recommended months for most Tibet EBC tours.
An 8-day Lhasa to EBC group tour starts from USD 923 per person. Private tours for 2 people start from USD 1,350–1,500 pp, dropping to USD 950–1,100 for groups of 4 to 6. Prices include Tibet permits, guide, vehicle, accommodation and entrance tickets; meals and flights to Tibet are not included.
Tibet's Everest Base Camp sits at 5,200m (17,060ft). Mild headache or dizziness is common. Our itineraries build in acclimatization stops in Lhasa, Gyantse and Shigatse, and all vehicles carry portable oxygen and AMS supplies.
Yes. Besides a Chinese visa, you need a Tibet Travel Permit to visit Everest Base Camp. WindhorseTour arranges this permit for you before your tour starts.
Most Lhasa to EBC tours take 8 to 10 days. The classic 8-day tour covers Lhasa, Yamdrok Lake, Gyantse, Shigatse and EBC. Extended tours of 10 to 15 days can add Namtso Lake, Yarlung Valley or Mount Kailash.
